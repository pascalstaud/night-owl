The Collection

Born from
Toronto after
midnight.

There is an hour in Toronto when the skyline stops performing. The CN Tower dims to a silhouette. The Gardiner empties. And the city becomes yours.

The Night Owl is a HOF and Drake collaboration built for that hour. Not a vehicle designed to arrive — a vehicle designed to move through the world on your own terms, in your own time. The exterior wears Midnight Black in a satin finish that absorbs streetlight rather than reflecting it. Beneath the surface, warm amber illumination traces the cabin architecture like the glow of a recording studio at 3 a.m.

This draws from Drake's nocturnal Toronto — the Bridle Path after dark, OVO Sound sessions that stretch until dawn, the quiet confidence of someone who has nothing left to prove. Every material, every detail, every decision speaks that language.

Design Details

Three decisions that define the collection.

01

Amber Pulse Lighting

Custom warm amber exterior accents replace standard chrome illumination. The light signature references Toronto at golden hour — except this golden hour happens at midnight. Subtle. Discovered, not announced.

02

Bridle Path Interior

Cream and cognac Nappa leather wraps the cabin in the warmth of a private residence. Dark wood veneer and brushed gold metal finishes echo the architectural language of the Bridle Path mansion — translated into four seats and a steering wheel.

03

OVO Owl Integration

The owl appears once. Debossed into the headrest leather, visible only when the light catches it at the right angle. No badges. No logos on the exterior. Discoverable luxury — a detail that rewards intimacy.

The Collaborator

Drake

Toronto-born, globally resonant. Founder of OVO Sound. His world bridges luxury and hip-hop culture, emotional nostalgia and forward motion.

The Night Owl is not an endorsement — it is a co-creation. Drake shaped the palette, directed the interior mood, and defined what this vehicle feels like at 2 a.m. on an empty highway. The owl is his. The restraint is ours. The result belongs to neither — and to both.
